Welcome, wanderer, welcome!

⚪️ Believe it or not, the hospital staff at the hospital where I was born took the liberty of spelling my name "Catherine" instead of the what my mother intended, which was "Catarina". I figure if I go by "Cat", it would be correct either way. I was born and raised in the heart of Texas, in a small town called Ballinger.
⚪️ I was raised in a very creative home. My mother painted, my father is a carpenter, and my two older brothers became an architect and a director. My brothers and I were classically trained at piano by the same piano teacher, Mr. Leroy Moore. I owe my strong attention to detail and dexterity to him!
⚪️ In the midst of piano, books and daydreaming, I developed a strong desire to learn about structures and art. So after high school, I moved to San Antonio to attend college and studied both Architecture and Interior Environmental Design from the University of Texas at San Antonio and the University of the Incarnate Word, respectively. The two subjects gave me the knowledge & foundationto go forth and begin my career as a designer. I had dreams of becoming a wonderous architect like Tadao Ando or I.M. Pei, both of whom I still admire.
⚪️ After graduation, I had the pleasure of working with the City Architect's office in San Antonio and got to work on some pretty cool projects. My favorite projects were the Tobin Center for the Performing Arts and the many renovation projects along the Riverwalk (which we got to travel to by the city's own river barge!). I sometimes could not believe what I was involved with at such a young age and valued every moment I had there.
⚪️ After the City, I worked with a San Antonio-based architecture firm which handled government projects. We completed renovations and new construction for military bases, air fields, schools, and various ports of entry along the Texas/Mexico border.
⚪️ A few years later, I relocated to Dallas to begin a new adventure as a 3D Branded Environment designer for a production company which produced live corporate events around the world. It was by far, the coolest job I have ever had. Our clients were many, but most notably we worked with companies such as: Tableau, Google, Apple, HBO, Lego, and so on. These live events were epic, for lack of a better word. Each year, these companies wanted bigger and better shows, sometimes feeling like a rock concert/carnival masquerading as a corporate event. We designed stages for their keynote speakers & musical guests, their LED lights, one-of-a-kind bar & coffee shops, ball pits, slides, instagrammable moments, and most exciting, a permanent Game of Thrones museum in Belfast, Ireland. It was incredibly amazing, at times extremely challenging, but all in all, the best experience I've had to date.
⚪️ Now that the world has halted a bit, it is time to turn my focus on something a little more different. I'm looking forward to seeing where this new knowledge will take me!
